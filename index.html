{{ site.cc-year }} Career Convention

As a junior, this is the time of your life when you should begin to explore what you are interested in and begin planning the steps to reach that career profession. By Fall of your senior year, you should begin applying for a school of choice, apprenticeship, gap year, or getting your soft skills in place to begin work.

If you are a senior and wish to graduate by the end of first semester, you must get your career paperwork turned in and it is completed correctly by the end of first semester. You CANNOT graduate without your state career requirements completed. Check with your school counselor on what that looks like this year.

Good luck! For any questions, check with your school counselor.

Mock Interviews

Pick two trusted adults, other than your parents, and set up a virtual meeting to complete the interview. Examples are lifelong friends of the family, teachers, church members, or a mentor.

You'll get a link in the app to send to your interviewers. Send them a copy of your resume and then set a time for the interview. You can do this over Google Meet or Zoom, too.

What needs to be turned in from the Mock Inteview:

  1. Resume
  2. Your reflection - you submit this through the app.
  3. Make sure the person who interviewed you sends in their evaluation of the mock interview. - they submit this through the app

Learn about careers

In the app you'll have opportunity to learn about careers you're interested in. There will be various videos from professionals in those careers for you to watch along with tips for how to learn more yourself.

You'll then fill out some reflections on the careers you researched.
