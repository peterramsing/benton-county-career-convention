The Benton County High School Career Convention is a partnership between the {% include partners.html %}

As a junior, this is the time of your life when you should begin to explore what you are interested in and begin planning the steps to reach that career profession. By Fall of your senior year, you should begin applying for a school of choice, apprenticeship, gap year, or getting your soft skills in place to begin work.

If you are a senior and wish to graduate by the end of first semester, you must get your career paperwork turned in and it is completed correctly by the end of first semester. You CANNOT graduate without your state career requirements completed. Check with your school counselor on what that looks like this year.

Good luck! For any questions, check with your school counselor.

Volunteers for Convention

We are seeking community members to get involved in this important event for eleventh grade students across our region. Volunteers can participate by:

  • Mock interviewing students
  • Participating in a career panel
  • Checking in and directing students
  • Being a community partner at a booth/table who can share services and opportunities with students (such as social services, internship or summer employment opportunities)

For booth/table reservation and general volunteering, please email Leah and Christa.

  • Christa Schmeder at Christa.Schmeder@corvallis.k12.or.us or 541-757-5850
  • Leah Tomlin at Leah.Tomlin@corvallis.k12.or.us or 541-757-5907

Volunteer Requirements

Important note: All career convention volunteers will be required to complete the Corvallis School District volunteer application.

Financial Support

If you would like to provide financial support for the Benton County Career Convention, please make a donation to the Public Schools Foundation and note "Benton County Career Convention" in the comment.
